About

A Photographer
with a Patient Eye

Photography, for me, is the art of paying attention. It started simply — a phone camera, a walk through Chittagong's old neighbourhoods — and grew into something I can't put down.

I'm drawn to the unguarded in-between: the way afternoon light pools on a harbour wall, a stranger lost in thought, the geometry of a skyline caught from the wrong angle on purpose.

My focus sits across street photography, portraits, and landscape — anything that rewards you for slowing down and actually looking.

The Other Side

Electronics &

By day I'm a final-year ETE student at Chittagong University of Engineering & Technology — navigating signals, circuits, and the inner logic of how information travels through the world.

The precision that comes with engineering has its own quiet influence on how I see things — but the two worlds stay separate, each with its own rhythm.
